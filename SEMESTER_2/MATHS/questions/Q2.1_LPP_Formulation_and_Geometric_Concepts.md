# Questions & Solutions: 2.1 LPP Formulation & Geometric Concepts

> **Topic:** Problem Formulation, Standard Form, Feasible Region, Convex Sets, Corner Point Theorem
> **Level:** Easy → Hard

---

## 🟢 Easy (Q1–Q9)

**Q1.** What does LPP stand for? Define it.

**Solution:**
**LPP = Linear Programming Problem.** It involves:
- **Objective function:** A linear function $Z = c_1x_1 + c_2x_2 + \cdots + c_nx_n$ to maximize or minimize.
- **Constraints:** Linear inequalities/equalities in the decision variables.
- **Non-negativity:** $x_i \geq 0$.

---

**Q2.** Identify objective function, decision variables, and constraints:
Maximize $Z = 3x + 2y$ subject to $x + y \leq 4$, $x \geq 0$, $y \geq 0$.

**Solution:**
- **Decision variables:** $x$ and $y$
- **Objective function:** $Z = 3x + 2y$ (maximize)
- **Constraints:** $x + y \leq 4$, $x \geq 0$, $y \geq 0$

---

**Q3.** What is the feasible region of an LPP?

**Solution:**
The **feasible region** is the set of all points $(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n)$ that satisfy **all constraints** simultaneously (including non-negativity). It is the intersection of all constraint half-planes.

---

**Q4.** Define a convex set.

**Solution:**
A set $S$ is **convex** if for any two points $P,Q\in S$, every point on the line segment $PQ$ also lies in $S$:
$$P,Q\in S \implies \lambda P+(1-\lambda)Q\in S \quad \forall\lambda\in[0,1]$$

---

**Q5.** State the Corner Point Theorem (Fundamental Theorem of LPP).

**Solution:**
If the feasible region is bounded, the optimal value of the objective function $Z$ occurs at one of the **corner points** (vertices/extreme points) of the feasible region.

---

**Q6.** Convert $2x + 3y \leq 12$ to standard form by introducing a slack variable.

**Solution:**
Add slack variable $s \geq 0$:
$$2x + 3y + s = 12, \quad s \geq 0$$

The slack variable $s$ represents unused resource capacity.

---

**Q7.** What is a surplus variable? When is it used?

**Solution:**
A **surplus variable** is subtracted to convert a $\geq$ constraint to equality:
$$x + 2y \geq 5 \implies x + 2y - s = 5, \quad s \geq 0$$
$s$ represents the excess over the minimum requirement.

---

**Q8.** What is standard form of an LPP?

**Solution:**
Standard form: Maximize $Z = \mathbf{c}^T\mathbf{x}$ subject to $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, $\mathbf{x} \geq \mathbf{0}$, $\mathbf{b} \geq \mathbf{0}$.

All inequality constraints converted to equalities using slack/surplus variables; all variables non-negative.

---

**Q9.** Is a single point a convex set? Is the empty set convex?

**Solution:**
- **Single point:** Yes, trivially convex (only one "segment" of length 0).
- **Empty set:** Yes, by **vacuous truth** — the condition holds for all pairs of points in $\emptyset$ (there are none).

## 🟡 Medium (Q10–Q18)

**Q10.** Formulate as LPP: A factory makes chairs (profit ₹400) and tables (profit ₹600). Requires: chairs need 2 hrs labor, tables need 4 hrs; total labor ≤ 40 hrs. Max 15 tables. Maximize profit.

**Solution:**
Let $x$ = number of chairs, $y$ = number of tables.

**Objective:** Maximize $Z = 400x + 600y$

**Constraints:**
$$2x + 4y \leq 40 \quad\text{(labor)}$$
$$y \leq 15 \quad\text{(table limit)}$$
$$x,y \geq 0$$

---

**Q11.** Convert to standard form: Maximize $Z=3x+2y$ s.t. $x+y\leq4$, $2x+y\leq6$, $x,y\geq0$.

**Solution:**
Introduce slacks $s_1, s_2 \geq 0$:

$$x + y + s_1 = 4$$
$$2x + y + s_2 = 6$$

Maximize $Z = 3x + 2y + 0s_1 + 0s_2$ subject to above, all variables $\geq 0$.

---

**Q12.** Find all corner points for: $x+y\leq4$, $2x+y\leq6$, $x,y\geq0$.

**Solution:**
Find vertices of feasible region:

- **$(0,0)$:** Intersection of $x=0$ and $y=0$.
- **$(3,0)$:** $2x+y=6$, $y=0 \implies x=3$.
- **$(2,2)$:** Solve $x+y=4$ and $2x+y=6$: subtract to get $x=2$, $y=2$.
- **$(0,4)$:** $x+y=4$, $x=0 \implies y=4$. Check: $2(0)+4=4\leq6$ ✓.

Corner points: $(0,0)$, $(3,0)$, $(2,2)$, $(0,4)$.

---

**Q13.** Prove that the feasible region is a convex set.

**Solution:**
Each linear constraint $\mathbf{a}_i^T\mathbf{x}\leq b_i$ defines a half-plane (convex). The feasible region is the intersection of finitely many convex sets (constraints + non-negativity). The intersection of convex sets is convex. $\blacksquare$

---

**Q14.** What is an extreme point (vertex)? Give geometric interpretation.

**Solution:**
A point $P$ in a convex set $S$ is an **extreme point (vertex/corner point)** if it cannot be expressed as a convex combination of two distinct points in $S$:
$$P \neq \lambda Q + (1-\lambda)R \text{ for any } Q\neq R \in S,\ \lambda\in(0,1)$$

**Geometrically:** Vertices are corners of the feasible polygon where two or more constraint boundaries intersect.

---

**Q15.** Formulate as LPP: Minimize $Z = 2x_1 + x_2$ s.t. $x_1+x_2\geq5$, $x_1+2x_2\geq8$, $x_1,x_2\geq0$. Put in standard form.

**Solution:**
Subtract surplus variables $s_1,s_2\geq0$:
$$x_1 + x_2 - s_1 = 5$$
$$x_1 + 2x_2 - s_2 = 8$$

Minimize $Z = 2x_1 + x_2 + 0s_1 + 0s_2$, all variables $\geq 0$.

(Note: Artificial variables would typically be added for the Simplex method.)

---

**Q16.** What is a bounded vs. unbounded feasible region? Give examples.

**Solution:**
- **Bounded:** Feasible region is enclosed (finite area). Example: $x+y\leq4$, $x,y\geq0$ — forms a triangle.
- **Unbounded:** Feasible region extends to infinity. Example: $x+y\geq4$, $x,y\geq0$ — extends to $+\infty$.

For a bounded region, both max and min exist. For unbounded, max may not exist (for maximization with $\geq$ constraints).

---

**Q17.** A diet problem: Minimize cost. Food A costs ₹2/unit with 3g protein; Food B costs ₹3/unit with 2g protein. Need $\geq12$g protein. Formulate.

**Solution:**
Let $x$ = units of Food A, $y$ = units of Food B.

Minimize $Z = 2x + 3y$

Subject to:
$$3x + 2y \geq 12 \quad\text{(protein requirement)}$$
$$x, y \geq 0$$

---

**Q18.** What is a basic feasible solution (BFS)?

**Solution:**
For $A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}$ with $m$ equations and $n$ variables ($n>m$):

A **basic solution** sets $n-m$ variables to 0 (non-basic) and solves for $m$ **basic variables**.

A **basic feasible solution (BFS)** is a basic solution where all variables $\geq0$.

BFS correspond to **corner points** of the feasible region.

## 🔴 Hard (Q19–Q25)

**Q19.** Formulate as LPP: A company produces 3 products with profit ₹3,₹5,₹4. Resources: 2 units R1, 4 units R2, 3 units R3 per product; limits 240, 470, 300. Maximize profit.

**Solution:**
Let $x_1,x_2,x_3$ be the quantity of products 1, 2, 3.

Maximize $Z = 3x_1 + 5x_2 + 4x_3$

Subject to:
$$2x_1 + 4x_2 + 3x_3 \leq 240$$
$$4x_1 + 0x_2 + 3x_3 \leq 470 \quad\text{(or as given)}$$
$$0x_1 + 0x_2 + 3x_3 \leq 300$$
$$x_1, x_2, x_3 \geq 0$$

---

**Q20.** Prove: Any convex combination of BFS is a feasible solution.

**Solution:**
Let $\mathbf{x}^{(1)},\ldots,\mathbf{x}^{(k)}$ be BFS's (corner points) with $\lambda_i\geq0$, $\sum\lambda_i=1$.

Let $\mathbf{x}=\sum\lambda_i\mathbf{x}^{(i)}$.

$A\mathbf{x}=A\sum\lambda_i\mathbf{x}^{(i)}=\sum\lambda_i A\mathbf{x}^{(i)}=\sum\lambda_i\mathbf{b}=\mathbf{b}$.

Since each $x_j^{(i)}\geq0$ and $\lambda_i\geq0$: $x_j=\sum\lambda_i x_j^{(i)}\geq0$.

So $\mathbf{x}$ satisfies $A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}$ and $\mathbf{x}\geq\mathbf{0}$. $\blacksquare$

---

**Q21.** Show the optimal solution to an LPP (if bounded) occurs at a vertex.

**Solution:**
Suppose $Z^*$ is the optimal value. Let $S^*=\{\mathbf{x}\in F: Z(\mathbf{x})=Z^*\}$ be the set of optimal points ($F$ = feasible region).

$S^*$ is a face of the convex polyhedron $F$ (an intersection of $F$ with a hyperplane), hence a convex polytope itself.

$S^*$ has at least one extreme point $P$ (a vertex of $S^*$). $P$ is also an extreme point of $F$ (a vertex of the feasible region).

Therefore, the optimal value is attained at a vertex. $\blacksquare$

---

**Q22.** Convert to standard form (with artificial variables): Minimize $Z=4x_1+x_2$ s.t. $3x_1+x_2=3$, $4x_1+3x_2\geq6$, $x_1+2x_2\leq4$, $x_1,x_2\geq0$.

**Solution:**
For equality $3x_1+x_2=3$: add artificial variable $a_1$.
For $\geq$: subtract surplus $s_1$, add artificial $a_2$.
For $\leq$: add slack $s_2$.

Standard form:
$$3x_1 + x_2 + a_1 = 3$$
$$4x_1 + 3x_2 - s_1 + a_2 = 6$$
$$x_1 + 2x_2 + s_2 = 4$$

Minimize $Z = 4x_1 + x_2 + 0s_1 + 0s_2 + Ma_1 + Ma_2$ (Big-M method).

---

**Q23.** Prove the intersection of two convex sets is convex.

**Solution:**
Let $S_1$ and $S_2$ be convex. Let $P,Q\in S_1\cap S_2$.

Since $P,Q\in S_1$ (convex): $\lambda P+(1-\lambda)Q\in S_1$ for all $\lambda\in[0,1]$.
Since $P,Q\in S_2$ (convex): $\lambda P+(1-\lambda)Q\in S_2$ for all $\lambda\in[0,1]$.

So $\lambda P+(1-\lambda)Q\in S_1\cap S_2$. $\blacksquare$

---

**Q24.** What is a degenerate BFS? Why is it significant?

**Solution:**
A **degenerate BFS** is a basic feasible solution where at least one basic variable equals zero (so more than $n-m$ variables are zero).

**Geometrically:** Three or more constraint boundaries pass through the same corner point.

**Significance:** In the Simplex method, degeneracy can cause **cycling** (visiting the same BFS repeatedly without improving $Z$). Bland's Rule or perturbation methods prevent cycling.

**Q25.** Transportation problem: 2 sources (supply 300, 500), 2 destinations (demand 200, 400). Costs: $c_{11}=2$, $c_{12}=3$, $c_{21}=1$, $c_{22}=5$. Formulate as LPP.

**Solution:**
Let $x_{ij}$ = units shipped from source $i$ to destination $j$.

Minimize $Z = 2x_{11} + 3x_{12} + 1x_{21} + 5x_{22}$

Subject to:
$$x_{11} + x_{12} = 300 \quad\text{(Source 1 supply)}$$
$$x_{21} + x_{22} = 500 \quad\text{(Source 2 supply)}$$
$$x_{11} + x_{21} = 200 \quad\text{(Destination 1 demand)}$$
$$x_{12} + x_{22} = 400 \quad\text{(Destination 2 demand)}$$
$$x_{ij} \geq 0$$

Total supply = 800 = Total demand ✓ (balanced).
